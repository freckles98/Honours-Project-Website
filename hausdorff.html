Hausdorff Distance

Project Goals

In this apporach, we use the Hausdorff Distance paired with and optimisation algorithm to identify planting patterns within trees. We use the centroid of each tree to represent the tree and identify patterns within the points. The Hausdorff Distance assesses the degree of similarity between a planting pattern and the data. Three different Hausdorff methods are used to evaluate the data and determine the best approach. The optimisation algorithm finds the best matches over the entire data set. The aim of the project is:


1. Evaluate the which Hausodrff method is best suited to the problem.
2. Determine the robustness and feasibility of the approach for determining planting patterns.

What is the Hausdorff Distance?

The Hausdorff distance’s objective is to find the difference between two points sets, by measuring how far the sets are from one another. The metric is fairly simple, given two point sets 𝐴 = {𝑥1, 𝑥2, . . . , 𝑥𝑛}and 𝐵 = {𝑦1, 𝑦2, . . . , 𝑦𝑚} the Hausdorff distance is defined as
(1) 𝐻(𝐴, 𝑏) = 𝑚𝑎𝑥(ℎ(𝐴, 𝐵) , ℎ(𝐵𝐴)) where
(2) ℎ(𝐴, 𝐵) = 𝑚𝑎𝑥 𝑥𝜖𝐴(𝑚𝑖𝑛 𝑦𝜖𝐵||𝑥 − 𝑦||)
(3) ℎ(𝐵, 𝐴) = 𝑚𝑎𝑥 𝑥𝜖𝐵(𝑚𝑖𝑛 𝑦𝜖𝐴||𝑦 − 𝑥||)
Where ||ℎ||acts as the normaliser. It is assumed in this paper that this is the Euclidean norm. (1) is known as the undirected Hausdorff distance and (2) and (3) are known as the directed Hausdorff [12]. To summarize, each point within the point set A determines the closest point in point set B and vice versa. Of those points, the maximum distance is found in point set A and the maximum is taken of each h(x, y) function. If the maximum is zero, then the points match perfectly. Algorithm

To implement the algorithm the following steps were taken:


Step 1
Creating model sets of of the patterns needing to be indentified. There were square, rectangle, quincunx, double row and heaxagonal.

Step 2
Using the polygon centroids as point and translating and rotating this on to an effective coordinate reference system.

Step 3
Determining the density of the data and scaling effectively to ensure 1 by 1 unit average between trees.

Step 4
Choosing the Hausdorff distance to be tested. There were three options: Modified Exact, Partial and Average Hausdorff Distance.

Step 5
Running the matching algorithm that performs rotations and translations of the model to effectively match it to the data.

Step 6
Repeating step 5 through the data set to find the pattern with the lowest Hausdorff Distance and thus the identified pattern.
